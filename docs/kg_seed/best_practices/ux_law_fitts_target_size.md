Fitts's Law: the time to acquire a target is a function of its size and the distance to it — big targets are easier and faster to hit, and small controls crammed together cause mis-taps. Apply it by making the primary CTA prominently sized and comfortable to activate, placing it close to where the user's attention/finger already is (e.g. directly after the headline flow), and giving secondary actions adequate spacing so they're not fat-fingered by mistake. On touch this reinforces the 44px minimum tap-target rule and argues against tiny, tightly-packed links inside a banner. Usability here is motor-ergonomic, not just visual: a beautifully styled but small or awkwardly-placed CTA still costs clicks. (Pairs with [mobile_tap_target_44px], [cta_button_contrast].)
