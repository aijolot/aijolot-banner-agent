Jakob's Law: users spend most of their time on other sites, so they arrive with mental models and expect yours to behave the same way — summed up as "don't be too original" with the basics. Reinventing fundamental interface elements (the example given: triangular text fields with weird buttons) leaves users lost, even if the result looks distinctive. For the banner agent this is the conversion case for conventional banner structures, recognizable CTA buttons, and standard layouts: spend creativity on the imagery, copy, and offer — not on making users relearn where the headline, button, or navigation lives. This is the behavioral mechanism underneath visual prototypicality: familiar = low cognitive load = trust and faster action. (Pairs with [hero_simplicity_prototypicality].)
