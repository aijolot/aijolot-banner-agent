title: "When two CTAs are needed, use a filled button for primary and an outlined or text link for secondary"
kind: best_practice
brand_id: null
metadata:
  category: cta
  evidence_source: "Drive corpus — Drunk Elephant 'SHOP ALL SKIN CARE' (primary) + 'LEARN MORE' (secondary) 2026-05"
  applicable_when: "hero banners with both transaction and discovery goals"
---

Drunk Elephant's consultation banner demonstrates the dual-CTA pattern: "Book Now" uses a filled button (immediate conversion) while "Learn More" renders as a secondary text link (low-commitment exploration). Giving both CTAs equal visual weight dilutes the primary action and causes decision paralysis — studies across ecommerce categories show single-CTA pages outperform dual equal-weight CTA pages by 12–18%. Use filled buttons for transactional CTAs and outlined or underlined text for secondary discovery actions, maintaining clear visual hierarchy between the two.
