Users form a stable aesthetic and trust judgment of a page in roughly 50 milliseconds, and that snap judgment is driven by two measurable traits: low visual complexity and high prototypicality. Visually complex heroes are consistently rated as less appealing and less trustworthy, while "prototypical" layouts — ones that look the way visitors expect a storefront hero to look — score highest because they cost the brain almost no processing effort. The implication for banner generation: prefer a single dominant headline, one clear CTA, and a conventional left-copy / right-image (or centered) arrangement over novel, ultra-creative compositions. Creativity should live in the imagery and copy, not in a layout that forces the user to relearn where to look. High cognitive load at the hero is a direct conversion tax — every extra element competing for attention lowers the odds the user grasps the value proposition in under 5 seconds.
