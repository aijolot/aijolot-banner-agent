High-converting product pages (Growth.design's Apple/Tesla-style teardowns) don't dump a wall of specifications — they intersperse small, focused feature-callout banners, each pairing one technical feature with the concrete benefit it delivers and a subtle supporting visual or micro-animation that reveals on scroll. These mid-page banners do three jobs: they break the monotony of body copy (so users keep reading), they convert abstract specs into reasons to buy, and they apply gentle persuasion principles — one idea per block (chunking), a visual that demonstrates rather than tells, and motion that draws the eye without nagging. Keep each callout to a single feature→benefit pairing; stacking many claims into one banner dilutes all of them. Use animation sparingly and never as the only way the information is conveyed (respect reduced-motion).
