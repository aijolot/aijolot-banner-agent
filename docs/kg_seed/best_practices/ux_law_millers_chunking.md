Miller's Law is popularly cited as 7±2 items of working memory, but the practical lesson is broader: "the more elements people have to process, the easier for them to make a mistake," and a busier screen raises the chance of error or outright abandonment. Treat the magic number as a reminder to reduce overall complexity, not a hard quota. For banners this means limiting each banner to one core message with minimal supporting elements, and for multi-step flows (e.g. configurators, lead forms behind a CTA) splitting the task into easy, logical steps instead of presenting everything at once. Chunk feature lists, trust cues, and benefits into small grouped units rather than one dense block.
