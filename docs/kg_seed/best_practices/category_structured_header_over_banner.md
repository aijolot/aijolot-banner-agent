GoodUI's A/B-tested pattern library shows that shrinking the decorative category banner and replacing the reclaimed space with a structured header — concise title, breadcrumb, and directly visible filters/subcategory tiles — increases click-through toward specific products rather than into a dead-end image. A massive banner consumes the most valuable screen real estate with content the user cannot act on, while structured headers give immediate, tappable paths deeper into the catalog. Treat the category header as a wayfinding device, not a poster: keep any banner imagery slim, lead with title + filters, and measure success by CTR into product/subcategory links. This complements Baymard's finding that subcategory navigation, not promotion, should own the top of an intermediary category page.
