  applicable_when: "all hero_main and top-of-page banners"

Banner blindness is selective attention: users instinctively skip page regions they perceive — correctly or not — as advertising, and NN/g eye-tracking heatmaps show fixations on ad-like blocks can be a tiny fraction of what their size warrants (in one case ~33× smaller than expected). Three signals trigger this avoidance: ad-typical placement (top strip, right rail), ad-like visual treatment (heavy animation, fancy framing, loud isolated color blocks), and proximity to real ads. A hero that adopts these traits gets dodged even though it is the brand's own primary message. Design the hero to read as page content: integrate it with the page's type and color system, lead with a genuine value proposition rather than a promotional shout, and avoid the bordered-box-on-colored-background "this is an ad" silhouette. The goal is for the user's eye to treat the hero as the thing they came for, not as something to scroll past.
