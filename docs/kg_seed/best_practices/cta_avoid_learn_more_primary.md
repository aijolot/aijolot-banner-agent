---
title: "Avoid 'Learn More' as the sole primary CTA on a conversion banner"
kind: best_practice
brand_id: null
metadata:
  category: cta
  evidence_source: "Drive corpus — Drunk Elephant uses 'LEARN MORE' as secondary, not primary 2026-05"
  applicable_when: "hero_main and promo_card banners with conversion goal"
---

Drunk Elephant uses "LEARN MORE" correctly: it is a secondary CTA that appears after the primary "SHOP ALL SKIN CARE". As a standalone primary CTA, "Learn More" signals to the user that no transaction is expected, which lowers purchase intent. When the goal is brand awareness or in-store consultation — as in Drunk Elephant's consultation banner — "Learn More" is appropriate alongside a transactional "Book Now". For conversion campaigns, replace "Learn More" with a product- or offer-specific verb that names the destination: "Shop Lip Essentials", "Browse Skincare".
