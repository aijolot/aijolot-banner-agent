---
title: "On category pages, never let a hero banner push subcategory navigation below the fold"

brand_id: null

Baymard identifies leading a category page with a hero banner that pushes subcategory navigation below the fold as "the single most common and most damaging mistake" on intermediary category pages — when the first screen is all promotion, a significant share of users never reach the navigation that would actually move them toward products. Promotional and editorial banners have a legitimate place on category pages, but only on desktop and only after subcategory navigation in visual priority. The category banner must not compete with or bury the catalog and nav: subcategory tiles should be the prominent above-the-fold content, with the promo banner as a supporting, secondary element. For the banner agent this means category-header banners should be sized as headers, not full-viewport heroes, and should sit alongside or beneath navigation rather than displacing it.
